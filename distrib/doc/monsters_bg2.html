Enhanced Monsters
This component brings all monsters closer to their PnP counterparts. For this purpose, I used many books, from 2nd to 3.5 edition.
For each monster, I checked:
- name
- general / race / class
- ability scores
- gender
- morale
- alignment
- thac0
- level
- hit points
- animation
- armor class
- attack per round
- saving throws
- resistances
- experience
- hide in shadow / move silently
- scripts (all replaced by better ones)
- items (weapons and special)
- effects (special resistances and immunities)
- movement speed
- proficiencies (if applied)
- spells and innates

Optional :
- high constitution affect monsters hit points. Currently, most monsters don't have a constitution score and even if they had one, it would make no difference as their class don't give constitution bonus hit points. This way, a 8hd hobgoblin would have the same hp as a 8hd mountain bear. For each monster, I added these bonus hit points in the following form : [+x]
- experience. I think that PnP experience is inconsistent and inappropriate in some cases. I tried to make a new system based on hit dice and difficulty level.

          Acidic Enzymes
          The ankheg can squirt a stream of acidic enzymes once every six hours to a distance of 30 feet. However, since it is unable to digest food for six hours after it squirts enzymes, it uses this attack technique only when desperate. A victim struck by the stream of acidic enzymes suffers 8d4 points of damage (half damage if the victim rolls a successful saving throw vs. poison).
          Whirlwind
          This whirlwind lasts for one melee round, sweeps away and kills all creatures under 3 Hit Dice in the area of its cone, and does 2-16 points of damage to all creatures it fails to kill outright.
          Dryad Charm Person
          Dryads have the ability to throw a powerful charm person spell three times a day (but only once per round). This spell is so powerful that targets of the spell suffer a -3 penalty to their saving throws. A Dryad always uses this spell if seriously threatened, attempting to gain control of the attacker who could help her most against his comrades. Dryads will only attempt to charm elves as a last resort because of their natural resistance to this type of spell.
          Petrification
          These reptilian monsters all posses a gaze that enables them to turn any fleshy creature to stone. Each round, all creatures looking at basilisk's eyes will be petrified.
          Foul Breath
          Greater Basilisk's foul breath is poisonous, and all creatures, coming within 5 feet of its mouth, even if just for a moment, must roll successful saving throws vs. poison (with a +2 bonus) or die (check each round of exposure).

          Incorporeal
          • No Strength score.
          • An incorporeal creature can be harmed only by magical weapons and spells.
          • Even when hit by spells or magic weapons, any damage sustained is reduced by 50% (except for positive energy, negative energy, force effects such as magic missile, or attacks made with ghost touch weapons).
          • Non-damaging spell effects affect the caster normally unless they require corporeal targets to function (e.g. Polymorph Other, Flesh to Stone, Disintegrate, and Implosion) or create a corporeal effect that incorporeal creatures would normally be unaffected by (e.g. Entangle, Grease, and Web).
          • An incorporeal creature has a base armor class of 0 due to its deflection ability
          • Incorporeal creatures have no weight and do not set off traps that are triggered by weight.
          • An incorporeal creature moves silently and cannot be heard.

          Breath Fire
          Hell hounds attack first by breathing fire at an opponent up to 10 yards away. The fire causes 1 point of damage for each of the hell hound's Hit Dice. A successful saving throw vs. breath weapon cuts the damage in half.
          Acidic Vapor
          The jelly monster secretes a vapor over a 10-foot radius. Those near the jelly must roll a saving throw vs. poison each round. Those who fail the saving throw become lethargic and move at half-normal speed, due to the effects of the vapor. The toxic effects last for two rounds and they are cumulative.
          Revenant's Stare
          If the revenant stares into its victim's eyes, that person must roll a successful saving throw vs. spell or be paralyzed with terror for 2d4 rounds. This power affects only the revenant's killer.
          Sphere of Fire
          Once per hour (6 turns), a skeleton may reach into its chest and draw forth a sphere of fire from the flames that burn within its rib cage. This flaming sphere can be hurled as if it were a fireball that delivers 8d6 points of damage. Because these creatures are immune to harm from both magical and normal fires, they will freely use this attack in close quarters.
